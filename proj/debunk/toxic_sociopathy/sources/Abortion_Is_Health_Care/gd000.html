World Health Chart, data sources

Data used in World Health Chart 2025 (pdf-version)

Version 2025.1

This describes the sources used in the World Health Chart interactive tool, and the PDF print out World Health Chart 2025.

— Countries: The graph shows 195 states recognized by the UN, which includes 193 member states and 2 observing states (with few exceptions, where important data is missing for year 2025: Holy See,  Taiwan).

— Color: The colors of the bubbles show the four regions, defined and color coded by Gapminder, which we use in all our graphs.

— Size: The area of each bubble corresponds to the number of citizens in the country, using estimates for 2025 from UN Population Division’s World Population Prospects 2024 published in the file with annually interpolated demographic indicators, accessed on July 29, 2024. For the animated chart, data before 1950 comes from many historic sources, documented in our Population indicator version 6.

— Y-axis: Life expectancy is based on estimates from Global Burden of Disease Study 2023 (GBD 2023) Results, Seattle, United States: Institute for Health Metrics and Evaluation (IHME), 2024. and UN Population Division’s World Population Prospects 2024 published in the file with annually interpolated demographic indicators, accessed on April 3, 2026. The animated charts use many historic sources, combined by Gapminder and documented in our Life Expectancy version 15.

— X-axis: Gapminder’s calculations of GDP per capita, in constant PPP dollars with 2021 prices. Based on World Bank’s data published in July 2025 in their World Development Indicators, extended with forecasts from IMF, published in World Economic Outlook 2025, July edition. 

— Income Levels: The vertical lines divide the graph into four income levels. Definitions, together with more details, are available here.
